Tenses in English



Parts of speech or sentence parts are one of the grammatical categories that group words in English with reference to their function in a sentence.

Simple Present

is a form of verb to express facts, habits, or events that are happening at the moment. This verb form is most often used in English.


  • Positive

    S+Auxiliary (do/does)+bare infinitive

  • Negative

    S+Auxiliary (do/does)+not+bare infinitive

  • Introgative

    Do/Does + S + bare infinitive

Example :

  • (+) She likes eating out
  • (-) She doesn’t like eating out
  • (?) Does she like eating out?

Simple Past

sentences used to tell stories about events that happened in the past and finished or ended in the past too.


  • Positive

    Subject + past tense + complement

  • Negative

    Subject + did + not + bare invinitive

  • Introgative

    Did + subject + verb

Example :

  • (+) The nurse went to the hospital yesterday.
  • (-) The nurse didn’t go to the hospital yesterday.
  • (?) Did the nurse go to the hospital yesterday?

Simple Future

Simple future tense is a form of tense to indicate an event that has not yet started and will only occur in the future, a.k.a in the future.


  • Positive

    Subject + will/shall + invinitive + Object

  • Negative

    Subject + will/shall + not + invinituve + Object

  • Introgrative

    will/shall + Subject + invinitive + Object

Example :

  • (+) Beni will go to Turkey next month.
  • (-) Beni will not go to Turkey next month.
  • (?) will Beni go to Turkey next month ?

Present Continuous

a form of tense that you can use to explain something or express an action that is taking place during a certain time in the present.


  • Positive

    Subject + Tobe (is, sm, are) + present participle

  • Negative

    Subject + Tobe (is, am, are) + not + present participle

  • Introgtive

    Tobe (is, am, are) + Subject + present participle

Example :

  • (+) Akmal is eating at the harum store.
  • (-) Akmal is not eating at the canteen.
  • (?) Is Akmal eating at the canteen ?

Past Continuous

Present continuous tense discusses events that are taking place in the present


  • Positive

    Subject + be (was, were) + present participle + Complement

  • Negative

    Subject + be (was, were) + not + present participle + complement

  • Introgative

    Be (was, were) + present participle + complement

Example :

  • (+) He was traveling to Venice.
  • (-) He wasn’t sleeping.
  • (?) Was he buying a book?

Present Perfect

a sentence pattern with a change in verb form that is used to express/express a past action/event that is still continuing until now.


  • Positive

    Subject + have/has + complement.

  • Negative

    Subject + have/has + not + complement.

  • Introgative

    Was/were + Subject + complement.

Example :

  • (+) You have been a creative writer since 2018.
  • (-) We have not been hungry.
  • (?) Has she been very busy this week?

Present Perfect Continuous

a verb form or tense to express an action that has been completed at a certain point in the past, or an action that has started in the past, but the action is still happening/continuing in the present.


  • Positive

    Subject + have/has + been + Present participle complement.

  • Negative

    Subject + have/has + not + been + present participle + complement.

  • Introgative

    Have/Has + Subject + Been + present participle + complement.

Example :

  • (+) Rara has been making cookies.
  • (-) They have not been helping your sister.
  • (?) Has your sister been driving?

Past Perfect

verb form to describe that there was an action in the past that was completed before another action (which was also in the past) occurred.


  • Positive

    Subject + had + past participle

  • Negative

    Subject + had + not + past participle

  • Introgative

    Had + subject + past participle ?

Example :

  • (+) I had finished my work before I went to bed.
  • (-) She had not studied for the exam before she took it.
  • (?) Had they finished their breakfast before leaving the house?

Past Perfect Continouous

a verb form to express an action (with a certain duration) or event that has started and is ongoing in the past, but is no longer happening now.


  • Positive

    Subject + had + Been + Present participle +Complement

  • Negative

    Subject + had + not + Been + Present participle + Complement

  • Introgative

    Had + subject + Been + past participle + Complement ?

Example :

  • (+) Rasya had been working at the international hospital for five years when he got the promotion.
  • (-) Lia had not been playing before she finished her exam.
  • (?) Had Alfi been working?

Past Future

a change in the form of a verb in English used to describe activities and events in the future, but using a perspective in the past.


  • Positive

    Subject + would + bare invinitive

  • Negative

    Subject + would + not + bare invinitive

  • Introgative

    Would + Subject + bare invinitve

Example :

  • (+) I would bring the book for you.
  • (-) I would not bring the book for you.
  • (?) Would I bring the book for you?

Future Continuous

verb form to express an event that is taking place in the future with specific time information, aka sentences that use this tense have a time duration.


  • Positive

    Subject + Will/Shall + Be + Present participle + Complement

  • Negative

    Subject + Will/Shall + Not + Be + Present participle + Complement

  • Introgative

    WWill/Shall + Subject + Be + Present Participle + Complement ?

Example :

  • (+) Rama will be running in a marathon race this Saturday morning.
  • (-) I won’t be sleeping at this time tomorrow.
  • (?) Will you be coming to Susi’s birthday party tomorrow night?

Future Perfect

vverb changes to state an event or state something that is predicted to have happened, even finished in the future. So, at the time it is being talked about, there is an action that has not been completed or has not happened at all.


  • Positive

    Subject + Will/Shall + Have + Been + Complement.

  • Negative

    Subject + Will/Shall + Not + Have + Been + Present participle + Complement

  • Introgative

    WWill/Shall + Subject + Hve + Been + Present Participle + Complement ?

Example :

  • (+) They will have been at your house when the pizza comes.
